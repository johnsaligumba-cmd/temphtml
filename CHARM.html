CHARM Project Orientation Hub

Climate, Environmental, Human Rights and Good Governance Action for a Resilient Civil Society in Mindanao and the Philippines

This hub is a central resource for project staff, providing essential information on project goals, frameworks, activities, and monitoring plans.

Project Overview

The CHARM project aims to empower Civil Society Actors (CSAs) in Mindanao to effectively monitor and advocate for the implementation of key international conventions, fostering a more just, sustainable, and rights-based society.

Overall Goal

To contribute to a more inclusive, peaceful, and resilient society in the Philippines through the enhanced promotion of human rights, environmental protection, and good governance.

Partners

A consortium led by People in Need (PIN), with the Manila Observatory (Klima Center) and the Bangsamoro Development and Peace Center (BMFI).

Target Groups

Engages at least 30 CSAs/CBOs, 20 activists, and 20 journalists, focusing on grassroots and marginalized groups in Mindanao.

Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL) Hub

The MEL Hub outlines our approach to tracking progress, ensuring accountability, and fostering adaptive management. This framework guides us in measuring our impact and learning from our experiences.

MEL Framework

Our MEL system is designed to be participatory and utilization-focused. It combines quantitative tracking with qualitative stories to capture the full picture of our progress towards empowering CSAs in Mindanao.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

We track specific indicators for each output, such as the number of CSAs trained, advocacy campaigns launched, and legal cases supported.

Data Collection Methods

Methods include pre/post-training assessments, activity reports, case studies, media monitoring, and participant feedback surveys.

Reporting & Learning Cycle

Progress is reviewed in quarterly meetings, shared in annual reports, and discussed in learning workshops to adapt and improve our strategies.
